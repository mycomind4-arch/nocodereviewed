Critical gaps the plan underestimates

HCU site-wide suppression risk
Google's Helpful Content classifier is domain-level, not page-level. One section of thin content can suppress your best articles. The 800-word floor in the original plan is too low — competing review sites at top positions average 2,400–3,800 words. New floors: reviews ≥1,500, comparisons ≥2,000, tutorials ≥1,000.
Critical
PBN footprint — identical Lovable structure
10 sites with the same nav structure, footer format, font choices, and component library are detectable by Google's crawler regardless of domain separation. Each site needs a different nav pattern, footer structure, font system, and color accent. Monthly visual audit: open all 10 side-by-side.
Critical
Voice drift by article 50
A 20-rule system prompt isn't enough — Gemini reverts to generic patterns fast. Fix: write 5 real articles per site manually, store as few-shot examples, append 2–3 paragraphs to every prompt. Also maintain a per-site banned phrase registry (seed: "leverage," "seamlessly," "game-changer," "delve into"). Drift reduction: ~70%.
High
Affiliate conversion rate too optimistic
The original 0.4% blended rate is realistic only for bottom-of-funnel content. Top-of-funnel traffic (40–50% of volume) converts at 0.05–0.1%. Realistic blended rate: 0.15–0.25%. Models should use 0.2%, not 0.4% — halves the affiliate projection but prevents false confidence in unit economics.
High
Missing revenue stream: paid research reports
Tool companies pay $2,000–8,000 for sponsored "State of No-Code" reports they use in their own sales cycles. At Month 6 traffic levels, you have the audience authority to sell 2–4/year per site. Potential: $40k–160k/year with near-zero marginal cost once the content pipeline runs.
Opportunity
Affiliate compliance risk
Most common violation: affiliate links without proper disclosure. Getting banned from PartnerStack and Impact simultaneously can crater acquisition value. Read every program TOS before publishing. Clean compliance documentation is worth $50k+ at exit.
High
Expired domain — critical sequencing error
A 301 redirect from the expired domain to a subdomain destroys authority transfer. The expired domain must BE the primary domain. Also: spot-checking archive.org misses months of spam. Run the CDX API on Wayback Machine for full URL history — any pharma/gambling/adult period = disqualify immediately.
