# VibeCode Authority Partner Governance Model

## Principle

Commercial relationships must never control scores, rankings, verdicts, benchmark design, or criticism. Revenue can support the ecosystem only when disclosures, separation rules, and audit checks are visible and enforceable.

## Partner Tiers

| Tier | Allowed | Blocked | Disclosure |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Directory only | Public listing, tool profile, non-promotional mention, and standard review eligibility | Paid ranking, winner badge, preferential scoring | No disclosure needed unless a commercial relationship exists |
| Affiliate | Tracked links in review sidebars, comparison tables, and best-of CTAs | Influence over score, verdict, ranking, test selection, or criticism | Affiliate disclosure before the first monetized link |
| Sponsor | Clearly labeled sponsor units, newsletter inventory, or report sponsorship | Review-card styling, implied endorsement, benchmark superiority claim | Sponsor label visible inside the unit and near any CTA |
| Research participant | Product demos, official clarification, bug response, and benchmark reproduction notes | Private correction of public score without logged evidence | Methodology note when vendor-provided evidence is used |

## Risk Controls

| Risk | Control | Audit |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Revenue bias | Scores and rankings are calculated from evidence records, not affiliate status | Compare monetized and non-monetized rankings monthly |
| Sponsor confusion | Sponsor placements cannot share editorial verdict-card styling | Check every sponsor unit for label, separation, and non-editorial copy |
| Vendor pressure | Corrections require source snapshots, benchmark reruns, artifacts, or factual error notes | Log changed verdicts with date, evidence, and reviewer note |
| Overclaiming CTAs | Commercial CTA copy cannot exceed the published verdict | Review CTAs whenever verdict, score, or readiness state changes |

## Intake Checklist

1. Record commercial relationship type and contact owner.
2. Confirm disclosure language and placement before links or units go live.
3. Map permitted placements to review, comparison, best-of, report, or newsletter surfaces.
4. Check the platform has evidence records independent of partner-provided claims.
5. Schedule monthly conflict audit across rankings, scores, and commercial placements.

## Hard Rules

- Paid relationships cannot buy scores, rankings, badges, or comparison wins.
- Affiliate links are allowed only after disclosure exists.
- Sponsored units must be visually and verbally separate from editorial verdicts.
- Vendor corrections require evidence, not preference.
- Commercial copy must never be stronger than the underlying review language.

## Operating Outcome

This model lets VibeCode Authority monetize without becoming a pay-to-rank directory. The site can still earn from affiliate links, sponsors, reports, and consulting, but the evidence system remains the authority.
