# VibeCode Authority Tool Intake And Coverage Model

## Principle

New platforms should not enter the review ecosystem just because they are loud. Every tool must be classified, qualified, benchmarked, and assigned to a coverage lane before VibeCode Authority publishes a verdict.

## Intake Stages

| Stage | Owner | Input | Exit |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Discover | Market watcher | Reader submission, category buzz, launch announcement, competitor mention, or search demand | Canonical name, URL, category hypothesis, and reason to track |
| Classify | Research editor | Product positioning, buyer intent, feature set, pricing model, and comparable tools | Segment, benchmark family, and coverage priority |
| Qualify | Benchmark lead | Official docs, pricing page, changelog, terms, and first hands-on notes | Required source snapshots and first benchmark task |
| Test | Benchmark runner | Benchmark prompt, account access, artifact requirements, and scoring model | Benchmark run, artifact, deploy/handoff notes, and failure taxonomy |
| Publish or park | Editorial lead | Evidence gates, QA result, market role, and audience value | Review, watchlist entry, comparison candidate, or parked item |

## Coverage Lanes

| Lane | Criteria | Examples | Action |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Core authority | High search demand, direct vibe-coding relevance, and strong buyer confusion | Lovable, Bolt.new, Replit Agent, v0, Cursor, Base44, Windsurf | Full review, comparison coverage, best-of eligibility, benchmark reruns |
| Adjacent evidence | Useful comparison context but not a direct prompt-to-app replacement | Bubble AI, FlutterFlow AI, Retool AI, Framer AI | Segmented review, alternative page, limited best-of inclusion with boundary notes |
| Watchlist | Early product, limited evidence, unclear traction, or narrow use case | New entrants, experimental agents, niche UI tools | Track signals, capture sources, delay verdict |
| Rejected or parked | Not relevant, too little public information, unsafe claims, or no testable workflow | Generic AI tools, undocumented launches, thin wrappers | Do not publish a review; optionally mention in market notes |

## Rejection And Parking Rules

- No public product surface or official documentation to verify.
- Not meaningfully related to app building, no-code, AI coding, or production handoff.
- Cannot be benchmarked with repeatable prompts or observable artifacts.
- Only vendor-provided claims exist and no independent evidence can be captured.
- Thin wrapper with no distinct buyer value or category signal.

## First-Test Assignment

1. Assign segment.
2. Choose benchmark family.
3. Capture source snapshots.
4. Run first benchmark.
5. Decide coverage lane.

## Operating Outcome

This model keeps the site from becoming a noisy directory. VibeCode Authority can expand beyond the first 28 tools while preserving evidence quality, category clarity, and ranking integrity.
