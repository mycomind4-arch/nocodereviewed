# VibeCode Authority Policy Governance Model

## Principle

Policy governance blocks risky public claims before they become recommendations. This is not legal advice; it is an editorial control system for disclosures, privacy notes, security caveats, pricing claims, and vendor corrections.

## Compliance Domains

| Domain | Risk | Required evidence | Blocks |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Affiliate disclosure | Readers may not know when links or recommendations are monetized | Partner status, affiliate URL, disclosure-ready QA, CTA placement record | Affiliate CTA, sponsor unit, best-value claim, monetized comparison winner |
| Privacy and data handling | Tools may retain, train on, or expose user/project data in ways relevant to production use | Terms/privacy snapshot, data-retention notes, training-data language, sensitive-data warning | Enterprise-ready, sensitive-data-safe, production-ready recommendations |
| Security posture | Generated apps may mishandle auth, secrets, permissions, databases, or deployment controls | Benchmark notes, artifact inspection, deploy notes, auth/data review, security caveats | Production-ready, pilot-safe, nontechnical-founder recommendations |
| Pricing and plan limits | Credit systems, plan limits, seat rules, or usage caps can change value recommendations | Fresh pricing snapshot, plan-limit notes, dated source URL, change-log status | Best value, cheapest, pricing table, affiliate CTA near pricing claims |
| Vendor claims and corrections | Vendor-provided claims can bias coverage or update verdicts without independent proof | Official source, independent benchmark rerun, correction note, reviewer decision | Score change, verdict upgrade, ranking move, correction publication |

## Human Review Triggers

- A page recommends a tool for production, enterprise, sensitive data, or nontechnical users.
- A page includes affiliate links, sponsor placements, paid report CTAs, or tool-submission offers.
- A pricing, privacy, security, ownership, retention, or training-data claim changed since last snapshot.
- A vendor requests a correction, score change, or revised conclusion.
- A report or best-of guide uses broad winner language across multiple tool categories.

## Required Disclaimers

| Surface | Required language |
| --- | --- |
| Reviews | VibeCode Authority publishes evidence-based editorial analysis, not legal, financial, or security advice. |
| Production readiness | Production-readiness notes are based on observable tests and source review; teams should run their own security and legal review before deployment. |
| Affiliate links | Some links may be affiliate links. Commercial relationships do not determine scores, rankings, or verdicts. |
| Pricing | Pricing and plan details can change. Each pricing claim should show the latest checked date and source. |

## Workflow

1. Detect risky claim.
2. Attach required evidence.
3. Apply disclaimer.
4. Run QA.
5. Approve or block.

## Operating Outcome

This layer protects the authority brand from overclaiming. It keeps production, privacy, security, pricing, and monetized claims tied to evidence and human review.
