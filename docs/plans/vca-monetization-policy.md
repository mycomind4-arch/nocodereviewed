# VibeCode Authority Monetization Policy

## Principle

Revenue must never change the scoring model, rankings, evidence gates, or final verdicts.

VibeCode Authority can monetize through affiliates, reports, sponsorships, submissions, and consulting only if every monetized surface is clearly separated from editorial judgment.

## Approved Channels

| Channel | Status | Allowed Placement | Hard Rule |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Affiliate links | Researching | Review sidebars, comparison tables, best-of pages | Affiliate status cannot affect score, rank, verdict, or evidence requirements |
| Benchmark reports | Planned | Free summary with paid PDF or data export later | Paid reports must show methodology, evidence window, and provisional labels |
| Sponsored placements | Future | Clearly labeled sponsor units | Sponsors cannot buy scores, badges, comparison wins, or conclusions |
| Tool submissions | Future | Expedited intake queue | Expedited review changes queue position only, not outcome |
| Stack consulting | Future | Founder stack audits | Recommendations must cite the same scoring model used on-site |

## Disclosure Requirements

1. Show disclosure before the first monetized link.
2. Keep sponsored units visually separate from rankings.
3. Do not use review-card styling for sponsor cards without a sponsor label.
4. Do not place affiliate CTAs beside stale pricing claims.
5. Do not publish monetized recommendations until the final QA disclosure field is ready.

## Editorial Firewall

The ranking order is determined by:

1. Benchmark evidence
2. Functional correctness
3. Production path
4. Code and data quality
5. Iteration speed
6. User fit
7. Evidence quality

Affiliate availability is not a scoring input.

## Blockers

A monetized page is blocked when:

- Pricing evidence is older than seven days.
- A recommended tool lacks benchmark evidence.
- Disclosure QA is not marked ready.
- The page claims a winner without comparable evidence.
- Sponsor content is not visibly labeled.
