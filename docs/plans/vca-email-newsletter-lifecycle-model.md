# VibeCode Authority Email Newsletter Lifecycle Model

## Principle

Email should compound trust. Every campaign must preserve verdict state, evidence freshness, disclosure state, and methodology access.

## Subscriber Segments

| Segment | Source | Promise | Primary Offer |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Founder Shortlist | Matcher, best-of guides, Lovable/Bolt/Replit reviews | Plain-language recommendations, pricing changes, and production-risk warnings | Vibe Coding Tool Shortlist |
| Technical Builder | AI IDE reports, Cursor/Windsurf/Replit reviews, production-readiness pages | Benchmark notes, code-quality findings, export risk, and handoff guidance | Production Readiness Checklist |
| Operator Buyer | Internal tool guides, comparison pages, Retool/Softr/Appsmith coverage | Permissions, workflow, data safety, and vendor-risk updates | Internal Tool Stack Brief |
| Research Watcher | Reports, methodology, readiness dashboard, corrections log | Monthly category movement, evidence windows, changed verdicts, and new entrants | Monthly Benchmark Digest |

## Core Sequences

| Sequence | Cadence | Goal | Required Proof |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Welcome and Trust Setup | Immediately + day 2 | Explain methodology, evidence gates, and what provisional verdicts mean | Methodology page, trust principles, disclosure policy, latest readiness state |
| Founder Shortlist Nurture | 3 emails over 10 days | Help founders choose between prompt-to-app builders without overclaiming | Best-of guide, current source snapshots, benchmark coverage, pricing freshness |
| Production Readiness Nurture | 4 emails over 14 days | Teach readers how to inspect auth, data, deploys, handoff, and security posture | Benchmark failure taxonomy, production checks, artifact examples |
| Monthly Benchmark Digest | Monthly | Summarize ranking movement, new evidence, stale claims, and corrections | Evidence window, changed records, correction notes, report QA pass |

## Newsletter Rules

- Every email recommendation must preserve verdict state: blocked, provisional, evidence-backed, or ready.
- Affiliate links require visible disclosure before the first monetized CTA in the email.
- Pricing, limits, privacy, and production claims require fresh source snapshots.
- Digest emails must include evidence window, changed verdicts, and correction links when relevant.
- Subscriber segmentation cannot override evidence gates or send stronger claims than the public page.

## Campaign Workflow

1. Select segment and offer.
2. Pull eligible pages, source freshness, verdict state, and disclosure state.
3. Generate or draft the campaign using the prompt governance rules.
4. Validate claims against public pages and source records.
5. Add correction links or stale-evidence warnings when needed.
6. Send only after disclosure and quality checks pass.

## Operating Rule

The email list should become the private briefing room for the public evidence system. It should never become a place where weaker claims are made because fewer people can inspect them.
