# VibeCode Authority Quality Gateway Model

## Principle

Autonomous publishing must stop when evidence, factuality, originality, SEO structure, or disclosure is weak.

## QA Thresholds

| Metric | Minimum | Blocks |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Evidence | 80 | Unsupported claims, missing artifacts, weak benchmark notes, missing source URLs |
| Factuality | 85 | Contradictions, stale pricing, unverified product claims, unsupported comparisons |
| Originality | 80 | Generic summaries, copied positioning, no original build observations |
| SEO | 75 | Missing intent match, weak title angle, no internal links, thin structure |
| Disclosure | Yes | Affiliate/sponsor links without disclosure or unclear commercial relationship |

## Rejection Reasons

An article, review, comparison, report, or build log is blocked when:

- Final verdict outruns available evidence.
- Pricing or feature claims are stale or uncited.
- The article repeats product marketing without benchmark observations.
- A comparison winner is declared without comparable runs.
- Production-ready language appears without deploy, auth, data, and handoff proof.
- Affiliate or sponsor CTA appears without disclosure readiness.

## Contradiction Checks

Before publishing, check:

1. Verdict vs evidence gates
2. Pricing claims vs fresh source snapshot
3. Winner language vs comparable benchmark runs
4. Production-ready claims vs deploy/security proof
5. Affiliate CTA vs disclosure readiness

## Operating Rule

Passing QA is not optional. If the latest QA check fails, the page can remain a draft or provisional evidence page, but it cannot publish final recommendations.
