# VibeCode Authority Distribution And Media Model

## Principle

Distribution must amplify evidence, not outrun it. VibeCode Authority can use cinematic authority-layer visuals for memorability, but public claims must remain tied to dated reviews, benchmarks, source snapshots, QA checks, and published methodology.

## Channel System

| Channel | Format | Cadence | Required evidence | Boundary |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Search | Reviews, comparisons, best-of pages, build logs | Continuous | Template evidence gates, internal links, freshness state, quality pass | No final winner language without comparable benchmark evidence |
| Newsletter | Weekly benchmark digest | Weekly | Changed source snapshots, recent benchmark runs, readiness movement | Digest can summarize movement but cannot create unsupported claims |
| YouTube | Benchmark walkthroughs, verdict explainers, failure reviews | Biweekly | Screen recordings, benchmark artifacts, deploy notes, disclosure state | Video claims must match the published written evidence record |
| Social cards | OG images, ranking cards, report announcements, result cards | Per publish event | Published page, dated report, or saved benchmark run | Cards cannot convert provisional guidance into absolute claims |
| Reports | PDF/data export, executive summary, score movement notes | Monthly | Dated evidence window, source records, QA pass, claim ledger | Reports must show methodology and evidence window |

## Asset Rules

- Authority-layer visuals are for covers, social cards, YouTube banners, report announcements, and launch moments.
- Evidence-layer visuals are for screenshots, tables, benchmark result cards, citations, and product comparisons.
- Do not use fake product logos, fake product screenshots, or unreadable synthetic UI as evidence.
- Every ranking card must link back to a page with gate status and methodology.
- Public media cannot make a stronger claim than the review, benchmark, or report it points to.

## Launch Asset Queue

1. Homepage OG card: authority-layer visual using the VCA institutional identity.
2. Core review lead images: one reusable authority image family for top platform reviews.
3. Best-of guide social cards: structured ranking cards with evidence-window labels.
4. Benchmark result cards: evidence-layer cards showing task, tool, score, and gate state.
5. Platform Index cover: report-cover visual with clear issue date.
6. Weekly digest image: lightweight evidence summary graphic for newsletter and social.

## Operating Workflow

1. Publish or update the evidence page first.
2. Confirm the page has a QA pass, source freshness state, and methodology link.
3. Generate the matching asset using the correct authority or evidence layer.
4. Publish distribution copy that links back to the evidence page.
5. If the underlying page changes, archive or update dependent distribution assets.

## Success Metrics

- Search pages produce qualified comparison and review traffic.
- Newsletter increases repeat readership and benchmark trust.
- YouTube proves the hands-on testing process.
- Social cards drive attention without creating unsupported winner claims.
- Reports become the commercial authority product for teams and agencies.
