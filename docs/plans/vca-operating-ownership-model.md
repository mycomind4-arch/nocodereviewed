# VibeCode Authority Operating Ownership Model

## Principle

Every publishing gate needs an owner, approver, and escalation path. Automation can move work forward, but it should not blur who is accountable for sources, benchmarks, verdicts, disclosures, and distribution.

## Roles

| Role | Owns | Approves | Escalates |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Research editor | Source snapshots, taxonomy, market classification, vendor claim validation | Official-source claims, privacy/terms notes, pricing summaries | Policy changes, ambiguous vendor claims, stale monetized claims |
| Benchmark lead | Prompt design, benchmark runs, artifacts, deploy/handoff notes | Score movement, benchmark comparability, failure taxonomy | Contradictory outputs, broken deploy paths, non-repeatable tests |
| Editorial lead | Verdicts, rankings, article briefs, final page structure | Review conclusions, comparison winners, best-of order, report findings | Unsupported strong claims, category-boundary issues, major verdict changes |
| Trust and policy reviewer | Disclosures, corrections, compliance triggers, sensitive claims | Affiliate surfaces, sponsor labeling, policy disclaimers, correction notes | Production, privacy, security, legal, or monetized recommendation risk |
| Growth operator | SEO, distribution, analytics, conversion, report packaging | Campaign copy, social assets, CTAs after evidence/disclosure gates | Distribution copy stronger than underlying evidence |

## Approval Matrix

| Workflow | Required approval | Blocked by |
| --- | --- | --- |
| New review publish | Research editor, benchmark lead, editorial lead, trust and policy reviewer | Missing evidence gate, stale source, unsupported verdict, disclosure failure |
| Comparison winner | Benchmark lead, editorial lead | No comparable runs, category mismatch, evidence parity failure |
| Affiliate CTA launch | Trust and policy reviewer, growth operator | No disclosure, stale pricing, unsupported value claim |
| Report publication | Research editor, editorial lead, trust and policy reviewer, growth operator | Missing evidence window, sponsor confusion, claim ledger failure |
| Verdict or ranking change | Benchmark lead, editorial lead, trust and policy reviewer | No evidence delta, vendor-only claim, missing correction note |

## Escalation Paths

- Policy or privacy change blocks publication until trust review resolves the claim.
- Benchmark contradiction pauses ranking movement until rerun or failure note is saved.
- Revenue pressure escalates to editorial lead when CTA copy exceeds evidence strength.
- Vendor correction requests require research editor evidence validation before score changes.
- Quality score below threshold sends the page back to drafting with blocked claims listed.

## Operating Cadence

1. Daily source watch.
2. Weekly benchmark batch.
3. Weekly QA review.
4. Monthly report review.
5. Monthly conflict audit.

## Operating Outcome

This model gives the ecosystem an accountable operating spine. It prevents the system from becoming a pile of automations with no clear owner when evidence, money, or verdicts conflict.
