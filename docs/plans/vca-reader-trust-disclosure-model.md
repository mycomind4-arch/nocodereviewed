# VibeCode Authority Reader Trust And Disclosure Model

## Principle

The trust system should be visible to readers, not hidden in internal process. Every important surface should show whether a verdict is evidence-backed, provisional, monetized, fresh, corrected, or QA-ready.

## Trust Principles

| Principle | Reader promise | Operating rule |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Evidence before verdict | Scores, winners, and recommendations do not become final until required evidence records exist | Use provisional language when benchmark, source, artifact, review, or QA gates are incomplete |
| Commercial separation | Affiliate and sponsor relationships do not control rankings, scores, verdicts, or criticism | Track partner status separately from authority score and disclose before monetized links |
| Visible revision history | Material factual corrections and verdict changes are logged instead of silently rewritten | Publish dated change notes when revisions affect recommendations, rankings, pricing claims, or disclosures |
| Category clarity | Tools are compared by use case and category, not forced into misleading broad rankings | State whether a comparison is direct, adjacent, or buyer-intent based before declaring a winner |
| Source freshness | Pricing, feature, privacy, and limits claims expire when official sources change or age out | Block stale pricing and production claims until source snapshots are refreshed |

## Disclosure Surfaces

| Surface | Disclosure required | Placement |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Review pages | Affiliate status, evidence completeness, last source check, and provisional/final verdict state | Near verdict module and before first monetized CTA |
| Comparison pages | Evidence parity, category boundary, affiliate status for both tools | Before winner callout and inside comparison summary |
| Best-of pages | Ranking methodology, evidence window, provisional entries, monetized links | Before ranking list and near CTA blocks |
| Reports | Evidence window, methodology, exclusions, sponsor state, and changed verdicts | Report cover/intro and methodology section |
| Social and video | Link to source page, evidence date, and provisional state | Caption, description, or card footer |

## Reader-Facing Badges

- Benchmarked
- Fresh source
- Artifact attached
- QA passed
- Disclosure ready
- Provisional
- Correction logged

## Display Order

1. Verdict state
2. Evidence badges
3. Disclosure note
4. Freshness date
5. Correction status

## Operating Outcome

This model turns transparency into a product feature. Readers should quickly understand why a recommendation exists, what evidence supports it, whether money is involved, and whether the conclusion is final or provisional.
